The Problem

Your portfolio company is approaching exit.

A 30-person AI startup with $3M ARR gets an acquisition offer. What's it worth? You check the databases. PitchBook says "undisclosed." Crunchbase says "undisclosed." The acquirer has done this 50 times. You've done it once.

The acquirer has the information advantage. You're negotiating blind.

The data exists. It's just scattered.

SEC filings reveal goodwill changes and aggregate M&A spend. HSR thresholds create hard price ceilings. Funding histories set floors. Patent transfers pin timing. Press reports carry estimates.

Each signal alone is weak. Triangulated together, they converge on a price.

How It Works

1

Mine

We systematically collect public signals for every acquisition in our target range: SEC filings, patent transfers, regulatory records, funding histories, press reports, and financial statements.

2

Triangulate

Each signal becomes a constraint on the deal price. Hard bounds (no HSR filing means price < threshold) intersect with soft anchors (funding multiples, per-employee values, press estimates) to narrow the feasible range.

3

Compare

When you need to value a company, we find the most comparable deals — by sector, size, deal type, and timing — and deliver a calibrated price range backed by traceable evidence.
